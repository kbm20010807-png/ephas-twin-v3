# TWIN PRIVACY POLICY

**Last updated: 1 July 2026**
**Effective date: 1 July 2026**

TWIN is a self-improvement and social application. Our AI coach, AXON, fuses your real-life data — from wearables, health signals, read-only finance, on-device voice check-ins, food logs, workout video, and in-app behavior — into a private "digital twin" that helps you understand and improve your life. This Privacy Policy explains what we collect, why, where it is processed, who (if anyone) we share it with, how long we keep it, and the rights you have over it.

We built TWIN local-first. Wherever we can, your most sensitive data is processed **on your device** and is never sent to us. We do **not** sell your data, we do **not** run advertising SDKs on your sensitive data, and we do **not** create a voiceprint. Please read Sections 4, 6, and 2 closely — they describe the choices we made specifically to protect you.

---

## 1. Who We Are and Scope of This Policy

1.1. **Controller.** TWIN ("TWIN," "we," "us," or "our") is the controller responsible for the personal data described in this Policy. TWIN is operated as a sole proprietorship pending incorporation. Our contact details are in Section 13.

1.2. **Scope.** This Policy applies to the TWIN mobile application, website, AXON AI coach, social feed, badges, courses, and subscription services (together, the "Service"). It does not apply to third-party services you connect to TWIN (for example, your wearable manufacturer, your bank, or your app store), which are governed by their own privacy policies.

1.3. **Home market and reach.** TWIN is offered first in the Gulf region (including Qatar) and then globally. Because we serve users in jurisdictions including the European Economic Area (EEA), the United Kingdom, the United States (including Illinois, California, and Washington State), and others, we apply a **strictest-common-denominator** standard: where laws conflict, we default to the most protective requirement for you.

1.4. **Roles.** For most processing, TWIN is a **data controller** (we decide why and how data is processed). Where we act only on your instructions — for example, computing an inference on device that we never see — the practical control rests with you.

## 3. How and Why We Use Your Data

3.1. We use your data only for the purposes disclosed at the point of collection, namely to:
- (a) build and maintain your digital twin;
- (b) provide AXON's coaching, insights, and reflections;
- (c) operate the social feed, badges, creator courses, and subscriptions;
- (d) secure the Service and prevent fraud and abuse;
- (e) provide customer support;
- (f) comply with legal obligations; and
- (g) improve the Service using aggregated or de-identified data.

3.2. **Purpose limitation.** We will not use sensitive data for a new, incompatible purpose without asking you again. Under the My Health My Data Act and PIPL, your consent is tied to a **specified purpose**; a change of purpose requires fresh consent.

3.3. **No profiling for ads.** We do not use your sensitive data to build advertising profiles, and we do not make solely-automated decisions that produce legal or similarly significant effects about you. AXON's coaching is a supportive tool, not an automated decision about your rights, finances, or health.

## 4. On-Device vs. Server Processing (Local-First)

4.1. **Our design principle.** TWIN is local-first. For sensitive categories, inference runs on your device by default. **On-device storage and computation means TWIN's servers do not see this data.**

4.2. **What runs on your device:**
- Voice-emotion / wellness analysis (raw audio never leaves the device);
- Workout-video form-check (video analyzed locally, not uploaded unless you save/share it);
- Local storage and fusion of health, food, and location signals for your twin, where feasible.

4.3. **What runs on our servers (disclosed plainly):**
- Read-only financial aggregation, which is inherently a cloud service via a licensed aggregator (Section 5.3);
- Social feed, badges, courses, subscriptions, and account management;
- Heavier AXON language synthesis that a device cannot perform, which may send **minimized, purpose-limited** context (never raw voice audio or raw video) to a no-training AI sub-processor (Section 5.2). Where this occurs, we tell you in-app before you enable it.

4.4. **Honesty commitment.** We will not claim data is on-device if it is processed on our servers. Where a feature crosses to the server, this Policy and the in-app disclosure say so, and name the sub-processor.

## 5. Third Parties and Sub-Processors

We share data only with the limited service providers below, each under a contract that restricts them to processing on our instructions. We maintain a current, dated list of sub-processors at the URL in Section 13.

5.1. **Cloud & infrastructure.** Hosting, storage, and database providers that run TWIN's server-side features. Data is encrypted in transit and at rest.

5.2. **AI / LLM providers (AXON).** Where AXON requires server-side language synthesis, we use commercial/enterprise API tiers that operate under a **no-training** contractual commitment. **Your health, voice, financial, and food content is not used to train third-party AI models and is not sold.** Inputs sent for trust-and-safety review, if any, are held only briefly by the provider and then deleted. We do not use consumer AI tiers that could train on your inputs.

5.3. **Financial aggregator.** A licensed, GLBA-compliant aggregator provides **read-only** access to your financial accounts. TWIN never obtains a scope to move, withdraw, or transfer money. Access is granted through your own **per-category consent**, is **withdrawable at any time**, and is revoked immediately when you disconnect the account.

5.4. **Analytics.** Privacy-respecting, aggregated product analytics on **non-sensitive** usage data only. **We do not run advertising SDKs on sensitive data, and no analytics or ad SDK receives your health, voice, financial, or food content.**

5.5. **Payments & subscriptions.** App-store and payment processors handle subscription billing under their own terms; TWIN does not store full card numbers.

5.6. **No onward sale or ad-sharing.** None of the sub-processors above is permitted to sell your data or to use it for cross-context behavioral advertising.

## 6. Data Sales and Cross-Context Advertising

6.1. **We do not sell your personal data** for money or other valuable consideration, as "sale" is defined under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA/CPRA) and the My Health My Data Act.

6.2. **We do not "share" your personal data for cross-context behavioral advertising** as that term is defined under the CCPA/CPRA.

6.3. **We do not run advertising SDKs on your sensitive data.**

6.4. Because we do not sell or share, there is nothing here to opt out of for advertising purposes — but you may still exercise all rights in Section 9, and we honor the Global Privacy Control browser signal as a valid opt-out request.

## 7. Data Retention and Deletion

7.1. **Retention schedule.** We keep each category only as long as the schedule in Section 2 states, or as long as needed for the purpose it was collected for, whichever is shorter. When a purpose ends, we delete or anonymize the data.

7.2. **Biometric / voice destruction schedule (published).** Consistent with biometric-privacy law (including the Illinois Biometric Information Privacy Act, "BIPA"):
- We do **not** create or store a voiceprint or biometric identifier.
- Raw voice audio is processed on your device and **deleted after processing or on your command**.
- To the extent any biometric-adjacent data were ever retained, it would be destroyed when its purpose is satisfied or **within three (3) years of your last interaction with the Service, whichever occurs first**.
- This retention-and-destruction schedule is public in this Policy.

7.3. **Account deletion (crypto-shred).** When you delete your account, we delete or irreversibly anonymize your data, including by destroying the encryption keys used to protect it ("crypto-shredding"), except for the minimum we must keep for legal, tax, security, or fraud-prevention reasons, which we hold for a limited, defined period and then delete. Unlike some platforms, we do **not** keep your data indefinitely after deletion.

## 8. Security

8.1. We protect your data with encryption **in transit** (TLS 1.2 or higher) and **at rest** (AES-256), and we use end-to-end protection for sensitive on-device data wherever feasible.

8.2. We apply access controls, least-privilege, logging, and secure development practices, and we minimize what leaves your device.

8.3. No system is perfectly secure. If a breach affecting your personal data occurs, we will notify you and the relevant regulators where required by law and without undue delay.

## 9. Your Rights

Your rights depend on where you live. We honor the **superset** of the following, and we will not discriminate against you for exercising any right.

9.1. **All users may:** access a copy of your data; correct inaccurate data; delete your data; export/download your data in a portable format; withdraw any consent (which does not affect processing before withdrawal); and manage per-category consent toggles in the app.

9.2. **EEA / UK (GDPR/UK GDPR):** rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction of processing, data portability, objection to processing, and withdrawal of consent. You may also lodge a complaint with your supervisory authority.

9.3. **California (CCPA/CPRA):** rights to know, delete, and correct; to opt out of sale/sharing (we do neither); and to **limit the use of your Sensitive Personal Information** (we already limit sensitive data to the purposes in this Policy). We acknowledge requests within **10 business days** and respond within **45 calendar days** (extendable once where permitted). We honor the **Global Privacy Control** signal.

9.4. **Washington (My Health My Data Act):** rights to confirm/access, delete, and withdraw consent regarding consumer health data. Consent for collection, sharing, and sale is kept **separate and distinct** — and we do not share for ads or sell.

9.5. **China (PIPL):** rights to access, correct, delete, port, and withdraw consent, and to give **separate consent** for any cross-border transfer (see Section 10).

9.6. **How to exercise.** Use the in-app **Privacy Controls** screen (per-category consent toggles with independent collect / share / sell switches, export, and delete-account), or contact us at the address in Section 13. We may verify your identity before acting. Authorized agents may submit requests where the law allows.

## 10. International Data Transfers

10.1. Because we operate globally, your data may be processed outside your home country. We use lawful transfer mechanisms per region:

- **From the EEA / UK:** Standard Contractual Clauses (2021 modules) together with a Transfer Impact Assessment, and supplementary measures where needed.
- **From China (PIPL):** your **separate consent** to the transfer, plus a valid mechanism (PIPL Standard Contract, CAC-recognized certification, or a CAC security review where thresholds apply). Where required, we conduct a personal-information protection impact assessment before transferring sensitive data.

10.2. You may request details of the specific mechanism applied to your data using the contact in Section 13.

# NOTES FOR YOUR LAWYER

The following are open items and drafting choices to confirm before publication. This document is a strong, compliance-aware draft, not legal advice, and must be reviewed by qualified counsel in each launch market.

1. **Controller identity / incorporation.** The Policy names TWIN as a sole proprietorship "pending incorporation." Once the QSTP (or other) entity exists, replace with the legal entity name, registration number, and registered address. A named legal entity strengthens contractual sub-processor terms and transfer clauses.

2. **Legal-basis election (GDPR).** I defaulted the sensitive categories to **explicit consent** (Art. 9(2)(a)) and non-sensitive to legitimate interests / contract. Confirm you want consent (vs. contract necessity) as the Art. 6 basis for core features; if a feature is genuinely necessary to deliver a paid service, contract (Art. 6(1)(b)) may be cleaner. If you rely on legitimate interests anywhere (2.6, 2.9), complete and retain a Legitimate Interests Assessment (LIA).

3. **BIPA / voiceprint.** The affirmative "no voiceprint / no biometric identifier / no identity verification" statements (2.2, 7.2) are the core BIPA defense and were placed deliberately. **Engineering must confirm the product actually does not generate or store a voiceprint or biometric template.** If any voice audio is ever server-side or retained, the published 3-year destruction schedule must be honored and a written-release consent flow added. BIPA carries a private right of action — this is the highest-litigation-risk item.

4. **On-device claims must match the build.** Sections 4 and 2 assert on-device processing for voice, workout video, and health fusion. **False "local-first" claims create deception/unfair-practice exposure (FTC, MHMDA, GDPR).** Have engineering sign off, feature-by-feature, on exactly what leaves the device, then reconcile Section 4.3 accordingly.

5. **Sub-processor naming.** I described sub-processors by category (AI/LLM, aggregator, cloud, analytics, payments) rather than naming vendors, to keep the Policy stable. Best practice — and PIPL/GDPR transparency — favors a **named, dated sub-processor list** at the linked URL. Populate 13.2 before launch (e.g., the specific LLM provider and its no-training tier, the financial aggregator, the hosting provider). Confirm each contract actually includes no-training, no-sale, and read-only-finance terms as stated.

6. **AI no-training commitment.** Section 5.2 promises server-side AXON runs on no-training enterprise/API tiers. Verify the executed contract/tier before publishing, and confirm the retention period for any trust-and-safety review data with the provider.

7. **Financial / GLBA & local licensing.** "Read-only, GLBA-compliant aggregator" assumes a U.S.-style arrangement. Confirm the aggregator's licensing and the applicable financial-data rules in **Qatar/Gulf** (e.g., QCB open-banking or sandbox conditions) — home-market financial regulation may impose additional consent, localization, or licensing obligations not covered here.

8. **PIPL / China.** I recommended possibly **geofencing China at launch** to avoid CAC security-review and CIIO/localization complexity given the sensitive-data volumes (the 10k-sensitive-individuals threshold is reached quickly). If you enter China, you need: separate transfer consent, a valid transfer mechanism, a PIPIA, and likely a local representative. Confirm the launch decision and update Section 10 accordingly.

9. **Transfers (EEA/UK).** SCCs + TIA are referenced. Execute the 2021 SCC modules with each non-EEA sub-processor and complete/retain the TIAs (Clause 14). Appoint an Art. 27 EU representative (and UK representative) if you have no EEA/UK establishment, and fill in Section 13.

10. **Children's age gating.** I set 16 (EEA/UK) / 13 (elsewhere). Confirm this against each market's rules (some EU member states set 13–16; some Gulf jurisdictions and app stores may differ). Decide and document your **age-verification method** — a stated minimum age without a verification mechanism is a known weak point.

11. **Response timelines.** CCPA 10-business-day acknowledgment / 45-day response are stated. Confirm your operational capacity (solo founder) to meet these and the GDPR one-month deadline; build the intake at the Section 13 address and the in-app Privacy Controls before launch.

12. **DPO / assessments.** Given large-scale processing of special-category data, evaluate whether a **DPO** is required (GDPR Art. 37) and complete a **DPIA** (Art. 35) for the health/voice/finance fusion — the DPIA is likely mandatory here and should exist before launch.

13. **Placeholders to fill:** entity name and address (1.1, 13.3); privacy email (13.3); sub-processor list URL (13.2); EU/UK and PIPL representatives (13.3); effective date if different from "last updated."

14. **Local Gulf/Qatar law.** This draft targets GDPR/CCPA/BIPA/MHMDA/PIPL as the strictest common denominator, but does **not** independently analyze Qatar's Personal Data Privacy Protection Law (Law No. 13 of 2016) or other Gulf regimes. Have local counsel confirm consent, transfer, and breach-notification specifics for the home market before publishing.
