## 1. HOW MUCH TO CHARGE

**The one-line answer:** Charge **$99.99/year (or $12.99/month)** for the main plan, with a premium AI tier at **$179.99/year ($19.99/month)**, a capped **$199 Founder Lifetime**, and a **$9.99/year EPHAS/student** price to seed your launch community.

### Why these numbers (vs the competitors)

| Reference point | Their price | What it tells you |
|---|---|---|
| Strava, Apple Fitness+, MyFitnessPal | ~$79.99/yr | The "default premium" floor. You should be **at or above** this. |
| Fitbit→Google Health, Bevel, Welltory | **$99.99/yr** | The market accepts ~$100/yr **once "AI" is the reason**. This is your anchor. |
| Duolingo Max, (AI tier) | ~2x the base (~$168/yr) | An AI power-tier at ~2x the base is a proven, high-margin play. |
| Noom (coaching + behavior change) | ~$209/yr | Coaching commands 2-3x meditation pricing. This is your **ceiling**, not Calm's $70. |
| Whoop | $199-359/yr **with hardware** | People pay this for a "recovery twin" — and you have **no hardware cost**. |
| Cal AI | $29.99/yr | The cheap viral floor. **Do not go here** — you do 5x the job. |

**The core argument:** TWIN replaces Strava + Calm + MyFitnessPal + a money tracker + a coach — roughly **$40-50/month** of separate subscriptions. At $12.99/mo you are not "another $13 app," you are **"one app instead of five."** That framing makes even the premium tier look cheap.

**Three rules baked into this card:**

3. **Lifetime is a scarcity/cash tactic, not a product** — capped, time-boxed, tied to the Founder badge cutoff, and **excludes unlimited voice/Opus** so one heavy user can't burn more in AI cost than they paid.

## 3. WHAT IT COSTS TO RUN (cost per PAID user / month)

Key structural fact you must internalize: **your expensive stuff (wearables, banking, voice) is FREE for all users, and only ~3% pay.** So the fixed costs get piled onto a small number of payers. This is why you lose money at small scale and make money at large scale.

### Cost table (blended, per PAID user/month)

| Cost line | @ 1,000 users (~30 paid) | @ 10,000 (~300 paid) | @ 100,000 (~3,000 paid) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Terra wearables ($499/mo floor) | ~$16.60 | ~$3.30 | ~$3.30 |
| Banking (Plaid ~$0.30/conn) | $1.50 | $1.50 | $1.50 |
| Hume voice ($0.064/min) | $2.56 | $2.56 | $2.56 |
| LLM coach (Gemini daily) | $0.53 | $0.53 | $0.53 |
| Claude Opus weekly (cached) | $0.20 | $0.20 | $0.20 |
| Food vision | $0.03 | $0.03 | $0.03 |
| Hosting (Railway) | ~$2.00 | ~$1.33 | ~$1.00 |
| Push (OneSignal) | $0 | $0 | $0 |
| Analytics (PostHog free tier) | ~$0 | ~$0 | ~$0.10 |
| RevenueCat (1% gross, ~free early) | ~$0 | ~$0.13 | ~$0.13 |
| **BLENDED TOTAL / paid user** | **~$23.89** | **~$10.10** | **~$9.99** |

**Read this:** at 1,000 users you *lose money on every payer* ($23.89 cost vs ~$13 price). The model only works past **~5,000-8,000 total users**, when the Terra floor and voice spread across enough payers.

### The AI-cost-OPTIMIZED number (this is the version to actually build)

The raw table above lets voice and Terra run wild on free users. Apply the six levers and the picture changes completely:

1. **Route 95% of calls to cheap tier** (Gemini Flash / local Ollama on your RTX 4080), Opus only for weekly synthesis.
2. **Prompt-cache** the persona + twin-summary (~90% off every call).
3. **Batch** the weekly Opus review (50% off).
4. **Cap free voice** to 1-2 check-ins/mo; put unlimited Hume behind Pro.
5. **Use HealthKit / Health Connect direct** (free) for the ~60-70% on Apple/phone sensors; reserve Terra only for Garmin/Whoop/Oura. **Cuts wearable cost 60-80%.**
6. **On-device** Whisper STT, embeddings, and form-check (your RTX 4080 = $0 marginal).

**Optimized cost per user/month:**
- **Free / light user: under $0.30** (text-only, cheap tier, heavy caching, no premium voice)
- **Pro user (with voice + vision): $1.50 - $3.00** — voice is the one line you must actively manage

This is the number that makes TWIN a genuinely high-margin business. **The AI is not your cost problem — undisciplined free-tier data collection is.** Gate Terra and Hume behind scale/Pro, and your true cost per paid user drops toward **$3-5**, not $10-24.

## 4. THE FULL P&L

**Two scenarios per tier: the honest "naive" case (all free features on for everyone) and the "optimized" case (levers applied). Build the optimized one.**

Shared assumptions:
- Blended ARPU: **$8/mo = $96/yr** (mix of $99.99 annual + some monthly + a few Pro).
- **3% of total users pay.** So "1,000 paid" = ~33,000 total users, etc.
- Revenue routed **web-first via Stripe** → you keep **~94%** (not 70% via app store). This is worth more than any cost cut.
- Fixed costs (solo founder): the founder's own draw + tools + legal.

### Break-even

- **Naive:** never breaks even — you must fix costs first.
- **Optimized:** each paid user brings ~$96/yr revenue and ~$48/yr variable cost = **~$48 contribution each.** To cover $35k fixed:
  **$35,000 ÷ $48 ≈ 730 paid users to break even.**
- 730 paid users ≈ **~24,000 total users** at 3% conversion.

**Break-even headline: ~730 paying subscribers (~24k total users).** That's your first real finish line.

## 5. HOW MANY USERS → HOW MANY PAID

Freemium conversion for a social app is **2-4%.** Use **3%** as your planning number (it matches your masterplan and is realistic for a distribution-first app).

| Total users | Paid @ 3% | Annual gross rev | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| 10,000 | 300 | $28,800 | Below break-even |
| **24,000** | **~730** | **~$70,000** | **Break-even** |
| 33,000 | ~1,000 | $96,000 | Barely profitable (optimized) |
| **333,000** | **~10,000** | **$960,000** | **Healthy — ~$1M ARR, 40% margin** |

**To hit 10,000 paying subscribers you need ~333,000 total users.** That's the target that makes TWIN a ~$1M ARR, ~40%-margin company on a solo founder + AI.

**Two ways to beat the 3%:**
- **Trial-to-paid in Health & Fitness is ~40% median** (best-in-class 68%). Run a 7-day TWIN+ trial fired right after the "First-Session Twin Bootstrap" aha. If you can push blended install-to-paid to **4-5%**, every point ≈ **25% lower cost per paid user** and dramatically better economics.
- Every 1% of conversion is worth more than any pricing tweak. **Conversion is the lever, not price.**

## 6. HOW TO BEAT THE MAJOR APPS

Your structural edge in one sentence: **ARPU stacks across 5 verticals while your cost stays flat, and the twin gets more valuable (harder to leave) every day the user feeds it.** Every competitor is single-vertical and hits a retention ceiling when that one need is met. You have five reasons to stay and a compounding data moat.

| Competitor | Their model | Their weakness | How TWIN wins |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Whoop** | Membership + hardware | Device COGS, upgrade backlash, supply chain | You have **no hardware** — same recurring ARR, zero device cost. "Your phone already has the sensors." |
| **Oura** | $349 ring + $6/mo sub | Users *resent* paying after buying the ring | **No $349 toll.** Ingest Oura's data as a neutral aggregator — be the layer *above* every device. |
| **Strava** | Freemium, social moat | Single vertical (sport only) | Copy the social graph (leaderboards/kudos = your leagues/badges) but across **all 5 domains** → higher ARPU. |
| **Duolingo** | Free habit + AI upsell | Language-only | Steal the streak/habit-first ordering and the AI-Max tier. Apply it to a **life** twin, not one skill. |
| **Cal AI** | Viral single feature | 30% retention ceiling, easily cloned, had to sell | Use their creator-army distribution (via EPHAS) — but your fused twin **structurally beats 30% retention**. |
| **Noom** | High ARPU coaching + GLP-1 | Human coaches are expensive, weight-only | AXON = a Noom coach across **money+body+mind at ~$0 marginal cost.** Add affiliate revenue on products the twin surfaces. |
| **Calm/Headspace** | Content subscription | Content commoditizes, both **shrinking** | Never be a content app. Fold "mind" in as one free tracker; your moat is the user's **own data history**, not replaceable content. |

**Three mistakes to never make** (learned from their scars):
1. Never paywall core logging (MyFitnessPal's barcode-scanner blunder).
2. Never tie a promise to physical hardware (Whoop's upgrade fiasco).
3. Never sell replaceable content (Calm/Headspace's negative growth).

**The 6-part money model:** (1) free addictive daily streak so the base is huge → (2) optional ads on free non-payers → (3) one sub that replaces 5 apps at $99.99/yr → (4) AXON Pro coaching tier at $179.99 (near-zero marginal cost) → (5) affiliate revenue on financial products/gear/supplements the twin recommends → (6) later, **B2B "TWIN for teams"** per-member-per-month (Noom/Calm's proven second leg).

## 7. THE MONEY-MANAGER SYSTEM (solo founder)

### The ~9 numbers on ONE screen (update monthly)
| Metric | Formula | Target |
|---|---|---|
| MRR / ARR | paying subs × ARPU (× 12) | grow 3-4x/yr early |
| Customer churn | customers lost ÷ start count | — |
| Revenue churn (track separately) | MRR lost ÷ start MRR | *never annualize ×12; 2%/mo = ~21.5%/yr* |
| CAC | S&M spend ÷ new customers | ≤ $45 |
| LTV | (ARPU × gross margin) ÷ monthly churn | ~$136 base case |
| LTV:CAC | LTV ÷ CAC | **≥ 3:1** |
| CAC payback | CAC ÷ (ARPU × GM) | **< 12 months** |
| Net burn | expenses − revenue | keep < $10-20k/mo |
| Runway | cash ÷ net burn | **12+ months** |

### Tools (start free, upgrade only when you outgrow them)
- **RevenueCat** — free under $2,500/mo tracked revenue; handles iOS/Android/Stripe subs in one SDK. Keep it.
- **A Google Sheet** — your P&L + runway. That's genuinely enough at bootstrap stage.
- **ProfitWell / Paddle Metrics** — free, for prettier cohort charts if you want them.
- Skip Baremetrics (~$129/mo) until well past PMF.

### The monthly "money day" (30-60 min)
1. Reconcile the business bank account, categorize every transaction.
2. Record app-store/Stripe payouts **NET** of fees.
3. Update the 9 metrics.
4. Recompute net burn + runway.
5. Take your **fixed owner's draw**.
6. Set aside the tax/payout-gap reserve.
7. Check one decision rule: *"if runway < 6 months, cut spend or raise."*

### Personal vs business money
- **Separate accounts from day one.** Open a dedicated TWIN account; route all payouts + expenses through it; pay yourself a fixed **owner's draw**. Commingling can pierce the veil and expose personal assets.
- Keep a **2-month buffer** for the app-store payout lag (Apple pays ~45-60 days after sale, net of commission). Web/Stripe pays faster — another reason to route web-first.

### When to incorporate
- **Now (bootstrapping, Qatar):** register in a **Qatar free zone — QSTP is the best tech fit**: 0% corporate tax on Qatar-sourced profit, 100% foreign ownership, ~2-4 week setup, local grants. Qatar has **0% personal income tax** and **no VAT** — a real edge for local web checkout.
- **Later (only when you have committed US investors):** form a **Delaware C-corp** (Stripe Atlas, $500), file 83(b) within 30 days, put yourself on 4-year vesting. Don't form one prematurely — it adds US filings and cost.
- **Tax on sales:** Apple/Google collect & remit VAT for you on IAP. For web/Stripe you're responsible — use a merchant-of-record (Paddle) or Stripe Tax where triggered. In the Gulf, add a **local gateway (Tap/Moyasar)** for mada/KNET/Apple Pay so you don't suppress conversion with card-only checkout.

## 8. THE BOTTOM LINE

**Is it profitable? Yes — but only if you build the optimized cost stack, and only at scale.**

- With naive "everything free for everyone" costs, **TWIN loses money at every scale.** More users = bigger losses. This is the trap.
- With the optimized stack (cheap-tier LLM routing, prompt caching, on-device STT/form-check, HealthKit-direct instead of Terra-for-all, capped free voice), TWIN is **profitable from ~730 paying users and runs at ~40% margin from 10,000 paying users** (~$1M ARR).
- **Break-even: ~730 paid subscribers ≈ ~24,000 total users.** That's the first finish line.
- **The real business:** ~333,000 total users → 10,000 paid → **~$960k ARR at ~40% margin**, run by one founder + AI.

**The single most important number to watch: FREE-TO-PAID CONVERSION.**

Everything hinges on it. Your expensive fixed costs (Terra, voice) are spread across your payers, so every 1% you lift conversion cuts cost-per-paid-user by ~25% *and* raises revenue at the same time. At 3% you're a modest business; at 4-5% (very achievable given Health & Fitness's ~40% trial-to-paid) you're a genuinely great one. **Price is roughly settled at $99.99/yr — stop optimizing it. Obsess over the conversion funnel and the daily-habit "aha" instead.**

*(Second most important number: annual-plan mix. Annual retains ~3x better than monthly. Default the toggle to annual, framed as "$8.33/mo," and never stop pushing it.)*
